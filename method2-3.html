CFOP Method-Step 3: Orient Last Layer (OLL)

The third step of the CFOP/Fridrich method is to orient the last layer (OLL). OLL solves the top face in 1 step, with 57 algorithms.

You should already know around 10 OLL algorithms, and a large chunk of the remaining cases can just be mirrored from other cases. Most algorithms are made from a combination of short sequences of moves called "triggers" (the parts in brackets), which make the algorithm easier to memorize. There is also a more advanced version of OLL in which you can simplify PLL if the top cross is made (COLL). However, COLL is beyond the scope of this guide.

Those who are unable to memorize this step can instead use 2-Look OLL from the beginner's method instead breaks the step down to 9 cases and sometimes even less, at the cost of a few seconds of solve time. I recommend learning full PLL before this step if you are already able to do 2-look OLL.

As OLL has way too many possible algorithms for each case to cover, this gudide will only display one algorithm for each case; remember that there are many more algorithms that you can use for a perticular case that you can find on the internet.
