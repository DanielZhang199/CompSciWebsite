CFOP Method-Step 3: Orient Last Layer (OLL)

The third step of the CFOP/Fridrich method is to orient the last layer (OLL). OLL solves the top face in 1 step, with 57 algorithms.

You should already know around 10 OLL algorithms, and a large chunk of the remaining cases are just variations of previous cases. Most algorithms are made from a combination of short sequences of moves called "triggers" (the parts in brackets), which make the algorithm easier to memorize. This is because there are much more limited things that you can do by this step.

If you can't or don't want to memorize this step, you can instead use 2-Look OLL from the beginner's method instead breaks the step down to 9 cases or less, at the cost of a few seconds of solve time. I recommend learning full PLL before this step if you are already able to do 2-look OLL quickly.

OLL has many different algorithms for each case; if you don't like the way a certain algorithms feels, remember that there are many more algorithms that you can use for a perticular case that you can find on the internet. The algorithms shown are just some of the more widely used ones, which are usually faster.
